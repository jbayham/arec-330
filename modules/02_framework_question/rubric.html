Lab 2 Rubric: From Question to Data

Total points: 100
Grading philosophy:

  • Emphasis on clarity of reasoning and engagement with the data
  • Partial credit encouraged
  • Penalize vague, unreflective, or copy-pasted responses

Section A: Formulating the Question & Decision Context (25 points)

Q2. Policy Question (10 points)

Score Criteria
9–10 Clear, focused, policy-relevant question that explicitly connects drought to corn productivity
6–8 Relevant question but vague or slightly misaligned (e.g., unclear outcome or population)
3–5 Loosely related; mixes multiple objectives or is too broad
0–2 Unclear, off-topic, or missing

Key indicators:

  • Mentions drought
  • Mentions corn outcomes
  • Answerable in principle with data

Q3. Policy Decision Being Informed (8 points)

Score Criteria
7–8 Clearly identifies a concrete policy decision (e.g., support programs, assistance, intervention)
4–6 General decision mentioned but lacks specificity
1–3 Vague description of “learning” with no decision context
0 Not answered

Q4. Meaning of Productivity (7 points)

Score Criteria
6–7 Correctly distinguishes yield vs production vs acreage and selects an appropriate measure
4–5 Mentions correct measures but shows some confusion
1–3 Uses “productivity” vaguely or incorrectly
0 No meaningful response

Section B: Data Needs & Conceptual Understanding (20 points)

Q5. Required Data & Unit of Observation (10 points)

Score Criteria
9–10 Clearly identifies key variables and the correct unit of observation
6–8 Identifies relevant variables but unit is unclear
3–5 Partial list of variables with little explanation
0–2 Very vague or incorrect

Q6. Evidence / Comparison Description (10 points)

Score Criteria
9–10 Describes a sensible comparison or relationship (e.g., before/after, across regions)
6–8 Reasonable idea but loosely articulated
3–5 Mentions data but no clear comparison
0–2 Not addressed

Section C: Understanding USDA NASS Data (30 points)

Q7–Q13 (scored holistically)

Score Criteria
26–30 Strong understanding of NASS structure, sources, aggregation, and limitations
20–25 Mostly correct with minor misunderstandings
12–19 Surface-level understanding; largely descriptive
0–11 Significant confusion or minimal engagement

Look for evidence of:

  • Correct unit of observation (state–year)
  • Awareness that NASS relies on surveys and estimates
  • Sensible explanations for missing values
  • Recognition of unobserved variation (e.g., farm-level practices, weather heterogeneity)

Section D: Applying Data to the Question (15 points)

Q14–Q15. Adjusted NASS Query & Drought Data

Score Criteria
13–15 Data choices clearly align with the policy question; datasets are well described
9–12 Mostly correct but incomplete descriptions
4–8 Data downloaded but weak connection to the question
0–3 Minimal or incorrect response

Section E: Working with R & Logs (10 points)

Q16–Q17. glimpse() Interpretation & Log File

Score Criteria
9–10 Correct interpretation of console output; log file runs cleanly
6–8 Minor misunderstandings or formatting issues
3–5 Log file exists but interpretation is weak
0–2 Missing or nonfunctional log

Section F: Reflection & Synthesis (10 points)

Q18–Q19. Reflection Paragraph

Score Criteria
9–10 Thoughtful reflection on decision → question → data process and limitations
6–8 Reasonable reflection but superficial
3–5 Mostly summary with little insight
0–2 Minimal or off-topic

Instructor / TA Notes

  • This rubric prioritizes reasoning over technical precision
  • High-scoring responses explicitly acknowledge data limitations
  • Encourage partial credit where students engage thoughtfully, even if imperfect
