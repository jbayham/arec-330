# Lab 12 Rubric: *Choosing the Right Visualization*

**Total weight:** 100% of the assignment grade  
**Grading philosophy:**

- Emphasis on matching the chart to the message, audience, and decision
- Reward short, clear explanations that show why a chart works
- Partial credit encouraged when the student has the right idea but weak execution
- Penalize visuals or explanations that ignore the decision context

## Part 1: Chart Basics and Chart Matching

### Q1. Best chart for a relationship between two quantitative variables (8%)

| Score | Criteria |
|-:|------------|
| 90-100% | Correctly identifies a point chart / scatterplot and explains that it shows the relationship between two numeric variables |
| 60-89% | Correct chart, but the explanation is brief or does not clearly mention why the chart fits quantitative variables |
| 30-59% | Mentions a plausible chart but mixes up the chart purpose or gives a vague explanation |
| 0-29% | Incorrect or missing |

**Key indicators:**

- Says point chart or scatterplot
- Connects the chart to two numeric variables
- Shows awareness that the goal is to see a relationship, not a category comparison

### Q2. Best chart for a comparison across categories (8%)

| Score | Criteria |
|-:|--------------|
| 90-100% | Correctly identifies a bar chart and explains that it compares categories |
| 60-89% | Correct chart, but the explanation is thin or generic |
| 30-59% | Partly correct, but the chart type or reasoning is muddled |
| 0-29% | Incorrect or missing |

**Key indicators:**

- Says bar chart
- Links the chart to comparing groups or categories
- Avoids confusing category comparison with a relationship plot

### Q3. Best chart for change over time (9%)

| Score | Criteria |
|-:|---------------|
| 90-100% | Correctly identifies a line chart and explains that it shows change across time |
| 60-89% | Correct chart, but the time-series explanation is incomplete or overly brief |
| 30-59% | Mentions a chart that could work, but does not clearly connect it to time |
| 0-29% | Incorrect or missing |

**Key indicators:**

- Says line chart
- Connects the chart to time or sequence

## Part 2: Agri-Environmental Decision Scenario

### Q4. Best visualization for the irrigation board (45%)

| Score | Criteria |
|-:|----------------|
| 90-100% | Prepares the data in a way that supports the decision. The chart clearly indicates the decision. |
| 60-89% | Mostly correct chart choice, but the data prep or justification is incomplete |
| 30-59% | The response shows some relevant analysis, but the chart is not well matched to the audience or the explanation is too generic |
| 0-29% | Little evidence of decision-oriented thinking, or the chart does not communicate the tradeoff clearly |

**Strong answers usually do all of the following:**

- Translate yield into crop value using the stated price assumptions, or otherwise put the farms on a common economic scale
- Compare the uniform cut and targeted cut on the same metric
- Choose a simple visual that a non-technical board can read quickly
- Use a chart that makes the policy tradeoff obvious, with the strongest answer being a direct comparison of the two allocation strategies
- State the one message the board should take away, in plain language

## Part 3: Apply the Idea to Your Own Data

### Q5. Context statement for the student-designed chart (10%)

| Score | Criteria |
|-:|--------------|
| 90-100% | Clearly states the problem, the decision, the decision-maker, and why that audience needs the chart |
| 60-89% | Mostly complete context, but one element is underdeveloped or vague |
| 30-59% | Gives some context, but the decision or audience is not clearly defined |
| 0-29% | Missing or off-topic |

**Key indicators:**

- Names a real problem
- Identifies the decision that must be made
- Identifies who is making the decision
- Explains why that audience needs the chart

### Q6. Final chart fit and explanation (10%)

| Score | Criteria |
|-:|----------------|
| 90-100% | Includes a chart that clearly fits the stated context and explains why it is the right visual for that audience |
| 60-89% | Chart fits reasonably well, but the explanation is brief or only partly tied to audience needs |
| 30-59% | The chart is present, but the fit to the audience or decision is weak |
| 0-29% | Missing, unclear, or unrelated to the context |

**Key indicators:**

- The chart matches the problem and decision
- The explanation mentions the audience explicitly
- The response justifies the chart choice, not just the topic
